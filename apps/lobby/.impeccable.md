# Pecking Order — Lobby

This is the design context for the **Lobby** (Next.js 15 app under `apps/lobby`). Lobby is the BEFORE: invite redemption, persona pick, waiting room, magic-link auth, playtest signup. It is a **distinct surface from Pulse** (the in-game shell). Polish work in this app should NOT inherit Pulse's calm-by-default voice or its Clash + Satoshi typography.

For Pulse design context, see the repo-root `.impeccable.md`.

> **2026-05-03 redesign:** The previous purple→pink→gold ramp was retired *for the lobby* (read as generic-AI default). The voice (`loud · gossipy · electric`) is unchanged. The color register flipped to a **tabloid B+W + red** family, scoped via `<html data-theme="reality-tv-tabloid">` in `apps/lobby/app/layout.tsx`. The default Reality TV theme (purple/pink/gold) is preserved in `theme.css` for other consumers of `@pecking-order/ui-kit` (Vivid, Classic, Immersive). Player-facing emails use a **paper-first** mirror of the same red palette (dark-mode email rendering is unreliable across clients). See `docs/reports/lobby-mockups/05-variant-a-welcome-v4.html` for the canonical visual reference.

## Design Context

### Users

Two audiences land here, often via shared invite links on phones during downtime:

1. **Invitees** — getting an SMS/DM saying "join my game", tapping a `/j/CODE` link. They've never used the app. Fast first impression matters more than any other surface.
2. **Hosts** — friends who created a game, sharing it, watching the cast fill, kicking it off.

Both groups are mostly teens / early-20s. Sessions are short (under 2 minutes for invitee onboarding; under 5 for hosts). Browser is mobile Safari or Chrome on Android. Entry context is the lock screen → notification → tap.

### Brand Personality

**Three-word personality**: *loud · gossipy · electric.*

**Emotional goals**: anticipation, casting-call energy, "you got picked." Lobby sells the show before it starts. Where Pulse is the broadcast (calm, dramatic, recede), Lobby is the casting reel + the dressing room + the door bouncer all at once.

The interface must feel:
- **Magazine-cover loud** — type that doesn't apologize, layouts that crop hard
- **Photo-driven** — persona portraits ARE the visual. Surfaces support them.
- **High-energy without jitter** — motion is welcome (party energy, live-event animation), but never decorative-for-its-own-sake. Animation should mark a moment, not fill empty time.
- **Friendly to first-touch** — invitees who've never seen the app should figure out what to do without reading

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme**: Dark-first for web (ink ground, paper text). Paper-first for email (paper ground, ink text). Same red accent in both.

**Visual tone**: **Tabloid magazine cover meets reality-TV title card.** Headlines crop hard. Persona portraits are the visual anchor; chrome is paper-on-ink (web) or ink-on-paper (email). Single red accent does identity, emphasis, and action. Loudness comes from **contrast and bold type, not hue.**

**Positive references**:
- Tabloid magazine covers — NYT magazine, People, Us Weekly cover grammar (bold type, photo-led, single accent)
- Reality-TV title cards (cast reveals, crop-hard composition)
- Newsprint chrome (ruled mastheads, monospace receipts)
- Premium recruiting flows (Cameo, BeReal — fast, photo-led, opinionated)
- Ticket-stub layouts for share / referral surfaces

**Anti-references**:
- The OLD lobby (purple → pink → gold ramp = AI-default tell)
- The Pulse shell (different product moment, different rules)
- Generic AI-default landing pages: glassmorphism + drop-shadow cards + cyan-on-dark + gradient text
- Enterprise dashboard aesthetic — gradient boxes, sparklines, multi-accent palettes
- B2B SaaS empty states with cute illustrations

### Design Principles

1. **The cast is the visual interest.** Persona portraits carry the page. Chrome surfaces stay simple — opaque, well-bordered, no glass stacks.
2. **Loud through contrast, not hue.** Big-type display headlines + tracked-caps eyebrows + bold rule lines do the work the old gradient ramp did. Single accent at a time.
3. **One red, three jobs.** Red carries identity (kicker eyebrow), emphasis (headline accent word), and action (primary CTA). Don't introduce a second saturated color. Gold survives only as a premium-signal accent and is used sparingly.
4. **Catfish is the load-bearing concept word.** Use it as the one term that explains the game on its own. Render in red when surfaced as the thesis. The "mask" metaphor is retired (didn't follow through anywhere else in the product).
5. **Mobile-first means thumb-first.** Every tap target ≥44×44. Bottom action bars use safe-area insets. Forms enable the primary button by default and validate on press, so users get feedback for every tap.
6. **One opaque card per page.** No card-inside-a-card. No glassmorphism stacks. Either commit to opaque chrome or let the persona photograph BE the card.
7. **Motion marks moments.** Persona arriving, button committing, badge popping in. Not infinite ambient breath on every card. One motion at a time, near the user's attention.
8. **Reality-TV verbs over UI mechanisms.** Player-facing copy names what the player DOES (`scheme`, `compete`, `betray`), not how (`chat`, `play`, `vote`). Mechanisms belong in italic sub-labels.
9. **No specific day count in copy.** DYNAMIC games have variable length. Use "each day", "day after day", "until only one catfish remains" — never `7 days` or `4 of 8`.
10. **Real-name privacy contract is explicit.** When collecting the player's real name, always say where it goes: stays hidden during play, players see the persona, revealed at the end.

## Locked Tokens

### Color (web — dark-first, in `packages/ui-kit/src/theme.css` `[data-theme="reality-tv-tabloid"]`)

A **lobby-scoped theme variant** that overrides the default Reality TV tokens with the tabloid palette. Applied to lobby via `<html data-theme="reality-tv-tabloid">` in `apps/lobby/app/layout.tsx`. The original `:root, [data-theme="reality-tv"]` block (purple/pink/gold) is unchanged — it remains the default for every other ui-kit consumer.

```
/* Backgrounds — ink ladder */
--po-bg-deep:    #0a0a0a   /* ink ground */
--po-bg-panel:   #131313   /* surface */
--po-bg-input:   #1d1d1d   /* raised surface (form field, bubble) */
--po-bg-bubble:  #1d1d1d
--po-bg-cartridge: rgba(10,10,10,0.92)

/* Text — paper ladder */
--po-text:        #f5f3f0   /* paper-white body */
--po-text-dim:    #a8a39c   /* muted */
--po-text-faint:  #6f6a64   /* faint, for captions/help */
--po-text-inverted: #0a0a0a /* on light buttons */

/* Single saturated accent */
--po-pink:       #d72638   /* tabloid RED — token name kept for codebase
                              continuity; semantically it is now red */
--po-pink-depth: #8a1a25   /* shadow tone for shadow-bevel CTA */

/* Premium signal — used sparingly */
--po-gold:       #f7c52e   /* gold accent for premium moments only;
                              never paired with red in the same component */
--po-gold-dim:   rgba(247,197,46,0.18)

/* Status (unchanged) */
--po-green:  #10b981
--po-danger: #ef4444
--po-info:   #818cf8

/* Borders */
--po-border:         rgba(245,243,240,0.10)  /* paper at 10% */
--po-border-active:  #d72638                 /* red for active states */
--po-rule:           #2c2c2c                 /* hairline divider */
```

The **token names stay** (`--po-pink`, `--po-gold`) for codebase continuity. The `pink` token's value is now red. Renaming is a follow-up; out of scope for the visual redesign.

### Color (email — paper-first)

Email clients render dark mode unreliably. Player-facing emails (invite, magic-link, playtest confirmation) use a paper-white ground with ink type and the same red accent:

```
--email-bg:     #f5f3f0   /* paper */
--email-text:   #0a0a0a   /* ink */
--email-rule:   rgba(10,10,10,0.16)
--email-accent: #d72638   /* red — same as web */
```

Emails inline these as raw hex in `style="…"` — no CSS custom properties survive the Outlook normalisation pass.

### Typography

Lobby ships on **Big Shoulders Display** (display) + **Manrope** (body), both via Google Fonts. Mono is **JetBrains Mono**, reserved for *actual* monospace need (codes, timers, technical receipts) — not for "tech vibes" texture.

- **Big Shoulders Display** — narrow, condensed, dramatic. Used for `--po-font-display`: hero `PECKING ORDER` wordmark, persona names, screen titles, all-caps eyebrow labels, rhythm-strip step labels (`SCHEME / COMPETE / BETRAY`), CTA copy.
- **Manrope** — geometric sans with rounded warmth. Used for `--po-font-body` and the page's default `font-family`. Reads warm, modern, not austere.
- **JetBrains Mono** — invite codes (`ABCD-EFGH`), referral codes, copy-link inputs. NOT for emails-prose, error messages, link captions, footers.

Email fallbacks (clients strip remote fonts):
- Display: `'Big Shoulders Display', Impact, 'Helvetica Neue Condensed', 'Arial Black', sans-serif`
- Body: `Manrope, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif`
- Mono: `'JetBrains Mono', Menlo, Consolas, monospace`

#### Type rules
- Display sizes use fluid `clamp()` for headings on hero / above-the-fold copy. Fixed rem on UI labels.
- 1.25+ ratio between hierarchy steps. No flat type pyramids.
- Body line length capped ~65–75ch.
- Cast names and screen titles are heavy weight + tight tracking. Subheads are lighter + open.
- No mid-weight gray text on colored backgrounds.

### Motion (Tailwind preset animations — unchanged)

`pulse-live`, `slide-up-in`, `glow-breathe`, `badge-pop`, `flash-update`, `count-pop`. Lobby allows these. Use one at a time per surface, anchored to user attention.

### Icons

Lucide icons. Lean toward heavier weights for display headers.

### Z-index

No formal layering token. Modals stack above; nothing else needs invented numbers. Keep flat.

## Patterns (use these across player-facing surfaces)

### Masthead
Two-element bar at the top of `/j/`, `/login`, `/game/[id]/waiting`, and any other player-facing landing page. Wordmark left, code receipt right. Underlined with a 2px solid `--po-text` rule. NO publishing decoration (no `Issue №007`, no `Casting Call · No.`).

```
[ PECKING ORDER ]                        [ CODE  ABCD-EFGH ]
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

The code receipt uses JetBrains Mono and a `CODE` label in faint paper-white.

### Rhythm strip (cold-player intro)
Three-step horizontal strip explaining what the player does each day. Lives near the top of the welcome surface, between hero and form.

- **Eyebrow** (red, tracked-caps): `EACH DAY`
- **Steps**: `01 / SCHEME` (alliances and rivalries) · `02 / COMPETE` (daily games for silver) · `03 / BETRAY` (vote one out)
- **Foot**: `Plays out day after day. Until only one catfish remains.`

Verbs are big-type display, sub-labels are italic Manrope, foot is small Manrope with `catfish` rendered in red. No specific day count anywhere.

### Cast fan
Fanned hand-of-cards persona portraits, 4 cards rotated `-9 / -3 / +4 / +10`, overlapping at `-20px`. Cards are 80×116, 1.5px paper-white border, slight desaturation (`contrast(1.1) saturate(0.85)`) so faces feel printed. **Single-line name strip** at bottom — name only. The persona `stereotype` field exists in the data; on the welcome surface it's NOT surfaced (too noisy). It does still appear on the `/join/` step 1 persona-pick UX where it has a job to do (helping the player choose a character).

### Verb stack (empty state on `/j/`)
4 alternating-color verbs in big display when no cast has joined yet. `Vote.` (paper) / `Ally.` (red) / `Betray.` (paper) / `Survive.` (red). Replaces the cast-fan visual.

### "Casting Call" kicker
Eyebrow above the headline on the welcome surface. Tracked-caps red with a 24px horizontal rule before the text. The thematic anchor — appears once per page.

### Footer-line
Closing line at the bottom of player-facing surfaces:
`One round a day · One leaves each round · Last catfish wins`

`Last catfish wins` is rendered in red.

### Real-name form pattern
On any surface that collects the player's real name (`/j/`, `/login` if applicable):
- **Label**: `Your real name`
- **Help text**: `Stays hidden while you play. Other players see your persona. Revealed at the end.` — `persona` is rendered in red.

### Wizard photo-bg treatment (`/join/`)
The wizard keeps the existing pattern of using the selected persona's full-body image as a blurred page background. Scrim color updates from purple-overlay to **ink-black at 0.65 opacity**. Hero text (persona name, stereotype, description) sits inside a defined hero card with its own bottom-up dark scrim band so legibility is deterministic regardless of photo brightness. No text floats over an unscrimmed photo region.

## Voice Rules

### Drop "mask"
The mask metaphor doesn't follow through anywhere else in the product. Don't use it on any new surface. Existing `mask` references in copy should be rewritten when the surface is touched.

### Lead with "catfish"
The one word that explains the game on its own. Use as both verb (`Catfish your friends`) and noun (`Last catfish wins`). Render in red on every player-facing surface as the load-bearing concept word.

### Reality-TV verbs over UI mechanisms
Big-type labels name what the player **does**: `scheme`, `compete`, `betray`, `ally`, `vote`, `survive`. Mechanisms (chat, DMs, silver-gifting, daily games) belong in italic sub-labels or implicit context.

### No specific day count
Never write `7 days` or `4 of 8 seats filled` in player-facing copy. DYNAMIC games run variable length. Use:
- `Each day` (eyebrow)
- `Day after day` (foot)
- `Until only one catfish remains` (foot)
- `One round a day · One leaves each round · Last catfish wins` (page footer-line)
- `[Names], [Names], [Names] are in. Tap in to join them.` (cast meta — names + invitation, no count)

### Headlines describe state accurately
- `You're invited.` (player has been invited but hasn't joined yet)
- `You're in first.` (no cast has joined yet — the player is the first to land)
- `You're in.` is **wrong** — it claims a state the player hasn't reached.

### CTA copy
`Step in →` for the primary commit on the welcome surface. `Lock In` on the wizard's persona-pick step. Active voice, theatrical, matches the casting-call register.

## Production Hardening Bar

Player-facing surfaces must handle:
- **Loading states** — skeletons, never just a "Loading…" string
- **Error states** — invalid invite, expired link, network failure, rate-limited
- **Empty states** — first joiner, all slots empty, no invites sent yet
- **Edge content** — long persona names (truncate gracefully, not mid-word), 280-char bios, missing portraits (`onError` fallback already in place — keep it)
- **Disabled states** — replace where possible with enabled + validation-on-press
- **Network failures** — server actions wrapped, errors surfaced inline, no silent swallows
- **Reduced motion** — `useReducedMotion` respected on entrance animations and ambient loops
- **Cross-device** — touch ≥44×44, `dvh` (not `vh`) for full-height containers, safe-area insets on top + bottom action bars, soft-keyboard doesn't crop primary CTAs
- **Image graceful degradation** — `onError` fallback in use, keep it; below-the-fold portraits lazy-loaded

## Migration Notes

The visual redesign is **lobby-scoped**, applied via the `data-theme="reality-tv-tabloid"` attribute on `<html>` in `apps/lobby/app/layout.tsx`. It is NOT a global token swap — `theme.css` keeps the default Reality TV theme (purple/pink/gold) under `:root, [data-theme="reality-tv"]` so every other ui-kit consumer (Pulse uses its own `--pulse-*` tokens entirely; Vivid/Classic/Immersive consume the default theme) is unaffected.

Most of the lobby work is then **token-driven**: setting the data-theme attribute cascades the new palette through every page that uses `skin-*` classes. Per-page surgery is still needed for:
- Copy updates (headlines, dek, footers — drop "mask", lead with "catfish")
- Composition tweaks (masthead pattern on `/j/`, `/login`, `/waiting`)
- New rhythm-strip insertion on the welcome surface
- Real-name privacy contract on the name-collection field
- Wizard photo-bg scrim color update (purple → ink-black)
- Cast-fan stereotype removal on `/j/` (kept on `/join/` step 1)
- Sweep of 3 lobby files that hardcode `#2c003e` (playtest layout `themeColor`, two refs in waiting page) — replace with `var(--po-bg-deep)`

The 2026-04-24 polish commit on `fix/lobby-join-polish` (legibility + layout pass on join + persona-wizard) is **superseded** by this redesign — its target surfaces are getting rewritten, not patched. Polish-commit logic that was per-element legibility surgery (font-size bumps, contrast fixes) becomes moot under the new tokens, but the *structural* polish (Step 4 reveal layout simplification, inline 56×56 headshot in Step 2, Q&A pair rendering) carries forward. The banned `border-l-2` on Q&A pairs gets the rewrite this redesign requires anyway.
