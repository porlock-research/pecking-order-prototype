# Pecking Order — Pulse Shell

This document captures the design context for the **Pulse shell** of Pecking Order. It is the source of truth for any `impeccable` / design-skill work targeting Pulse. Other shells (Classic, Immersive, Vivid) are out of scope for this file — they have their own histories and are not where the product is heading.

## Design Context

### Users

Teenagers playing Pecking Order, a 7-day persistent social deduction game with friends and strangers. They're on their phones during downtime — lunch breaks, between classes, late at night in bed. Sessions are short and frequent. The job they're hiring Pulse for is to feel the drama of a reality-TV cast they're actually part of: gossip, alliances, betrayals, and daily social rhythms. Chat is the primary surface; everything else (voting, mini-games, activities, reveals) interrupts the conversation as dramatic events.

### Brand Personality

**Three-word personality**: *tactile · social · dramatic.*

**Emotional goals**: intimacy, FOMO, and charge. Every surface should feel aware of game state and respond to social moments. Think group chat in a nightclub VIP section — the app is the room, the players are the cast, and you're watching something happen in real time.

The interface must feel:
- **Slick** — modern, premium, game-like (not utility, not enterprise)
- **Alive** — reactive to activity, not a static document
- **Touch-responsive** — every tap has tactile feedback
- **Character-first** — persona photos provide all the color; UI chrome recedes

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme**: Dark-first (warm ink, not pure black). Chosen because teens use this at night and in brief idle moments; dark keeps the persona photos primary and is the context in which "reality TV drama" lives.

**Visual tone**: Warm maximalism in *content* (portraits, ignition beats, gold/pink accents on dramatic moments) + refined minimalism in *chrome* (surfaces, borders, typography). Characters ARE the visual interest.

**Anti-references**:
- The **Vivid shell** in this same repo — teens called it "boring." Vivid borrowed enterprise-messaging patterns (BroadcastBar, ticker, dramatic reveals that over-announced themselves). Pulse must be visually distinct, never a reskin. Reference Vivid for data flow only; never for UI patterns.
- Enterprise / admin / utility aesthetics — gradient-heavy, border-heavy, multi-accent palettes.
- Generic AI-design tells — glassmorphism everywhere, cyan-on-dark, purple-to-blue gradients, gradient text, colored side-stripes on cards, sparklines as decoration, icon-above-heading cards.

**Positive reference**: No single app captures it. Closest cousins are reality-TV title cards (bold, character-rich type), premium group-chat moments on Discord / iMessage, and nightclub visual codes (warm ink, gold flash, pink charge). Build the reference, don't borrow one.

### Design Principles

1. **The cast is the visual interest.** Persona photos provide the color. Surfaces recede to near-black. One accent (pink), one signal (gold), everything else is warm neutral — 60/30/10.
2. **Chrome recedes, moments emerge.** Static state is calm and spacious. Drama (silver sends, votes, reveals) breaks through with an ignition beat — one bold 300–500ms moment, then settle.
3. **Chat is the primary surface.** Voting, games, DMs, reveals are all interruptions to the conversation — they should feel like dramatic events, never dashboards.
4. **Every tap responds.** Spring physics, not duration tweens. Optimistic UI. No dead taps on pills, avatars, cartridge cards, or content.
5. **Calm by default, loud on purpose.** Only pending/urgent state pulses ambiently. Decorative motion is banned. Reserve the 10% accent weight for moments that matter.

## Locked Tokens (already shipped — do not redesign without reason)

### Color (`pulse-theme.css`)
- Warm ink ladder: `#15121a` → `#1a1420` → `#1d1826` → `#231c2d` → `#2a2335` (bg → surface tiers). Never pure black.
- Primary accent: **hot pink** `#ff3b6f` (`--pulse-accent`)
- Signal: **gold** `#ffc83d` (`--pulse-gold`) — reserved for silver/premium moments
- Per-cartridge hues (vote=green, game=amber, prompt=blue, dilemma=purple, whisper=violet, nudge=orange) — used sparingly as type-identity, not decoration
- One visible accent at a time. Pink + gold are the 10%.

### Motion (`springs.ts`)
`PULSE_SPRING.bouncy | snappy | gentle | page | pop | exit`. Raw cubic-beziers banned at call sites. Calm by default, event-driven, single ignition beat per moment.

### Icons
Phosphor Icons, **Fill weight**, via `shells/pulse/icons.ts`. Chunky + playful, reads well at small sizes. No emoji.

### Z-index
`PULSE_Z` tiers in `shells/pulse/zIndex.ts`: `base → flow → elevated → popup → reactionBar → drawer → modal → reveal`. Never invent raw numbers.

## Open Decisions

### Typography — revisit
Pulse currently ships on **Outfit** (all weights). Outfit is on impeccable's trained-reflex reject list — it's a default that creates cross-project monoculture. The pick is open for a swap.

Directional brief for a replacement:
- **Display** should feel like a reality-TV title card or a music-festival lanyard — bold, a little opinionated, character-rich. Not a neutral grotesque.
- **Body** should be clean and modern but *warm* — not the austere IBM-Plex / Inter register.
- **Pair** a distinctive display face with a refined body face; don't use one family for everything.

Candidate pairings to explore (none committed):
- **Clash Display** + **Satoshi** — modular, bold, teen-friendly
- **PP Neue Machina** + **Neue Montreal** — technical/editorial
- **Boldonse** + **General Sans** — display pop + clean body
- **PP Editorial New** + **Söhne** — editorial/magazine energy

Decide with the brief above, not the reflex.

### Accessibility
No formal floor. Keep body-text contrast reasonable (~4.5:1 on warm ink). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for ignition beats. Don't over-constrain decisions for compliance when the product has no compliance target.
