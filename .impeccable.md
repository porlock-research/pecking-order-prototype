# Pecking Order — Pulse Shell

This document captures the design context for the **Pulse shell** of Pecking Order. It is the source of truth for any `impeccable` / design-skill work targeting Pulse. Other shells (Classic, Immersive, Vivid) are out of scope for this file — they have their own histories and are not where the product is heading.

## Design Context

### Users

Teenagers playing Pecking Order, a 7-day persistent social deduction game with friends and strangers. They're on their phones during downtime — lunch breaks, between classes, late at night in bed. Sessions are short and frequent. The job they're hiring Pulse for is to feel the drama of a reality-TV cast they're actually part of: gossip, alliances, betrayals, and daily social rhythms. Chat is the primary surface; everything else (voting, mini-games, activities, reveals) interrupts the conversation as dramatic events.

**Async-first**: players aren't connected in real time. State-change moments (eliminations, game results, silver sends) arrive as push notifications; the player opens the app *after* the event and re-watches the moment. This means every dramatic reveal is a **replay, not a live event**. Design for the player landing cold on a finished outcome — not for synchronized group attention.

### Brand Personality

**Three-word personality**: *tactile · social · dramatic.*

**Emotional goals**: intimacy, FOMO, and charge. Every surface should feel aware of game state and respond to social moments. Think group chat in a nightclub VIP section — the app is the room, the players are the cast, and you're watching something happen in real time.

The interface must feel:
- **Slick** — modern, premium, game-like (not utility, not enterprise)
- **Alive** — reactive to activity, not a static document
- **Touch-responsive** — every tap has tactile feedback
- **Character-first** — persona photos provide all the color; UI chrome recedes

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme**: Dark-first (warm ink, not pure black). Chosen because teens use this at night and in brief idle moments; dark keeps the persona photos primary and is the context in which "reality TV drama" lives.

**Visual tone**: Warm maximalism in *content* (portraits, ignition beats, gold/pink accents on dramatic moments) + refined minimalism in *chrome* (surfaces, borders, typography). Characters ARE the visual interest.

**Anti-references**:
- The **Vivid shell** in this same repo — teens called it "boring." Vivid borrowed enterprise-messaging patterns (BroadcastBar, ticker, dramatic reveals that over-announced themselves). Pulse must be visually distinct, never a reskin. Reference Vivid for data flow only; never for UI patterns.
- Enterprise / admin / utility aesthetics — gradient-heavy, border-heavy, multi-accent palettes.
- Generic AI-design tells — glassmorphism everywhere, cyan-on-dark, purple-to-blue gradients, gradient text, colored side-stripes on cards, sparklines as decoration, icon-above-heading cards.

**Positive reference**: No single app captures it. Closest cousins are reality-TV title cards (bold, character-rich type), premium group-chat moments on Discord / iMessage, and nightclub visual codes (warm ink, gold flash, pink charge). Build the reference, don't borrow one.

### Design Principles

1. **The cast is the visual interest.** Persona photos provide the color. Surfaces recede to near-black. One accent (pink), one signal (gold), everything else is warm neutral — 60/30/10.
2. **Chrome recedes, moments emerge.** Static state is calm and spacious. Drama (silver sends, votes, reveals) breaks through with an ignition beat — one bold 300–500ms moment, then settle.
3. **Chat is the primary surface.** Voting, games, DMs, reveals are all interruptions to the conversation — they should feel like dramatic events, never dashboards.
4. **Every tap responds.** Spring physics, not duration tweens. Optimistic UI. No dead taps on pills, avatars, cartridge cards, or content.
5. **Calm by default, loud on purpose.** Only pending/urgent state pulses ambiently. Decorative motion is banned. Reserve the 10% accent weight for moments that matter.
6. **Reveals are replays.** Every dramatic moment is a self-contained reel that plays edge-to-edge on mount and resolves to a still, screenshot-ready peak frame. Animations compose toward a composition that reads even when frozen. Tap the moment to replay it — the drama should feel re-runnable, not one-shot.
7. **Commit actions have undo windows.** Irreversible taps (voting, sending silver, locking a choice) get a 3-second spring-backed countdown with tap-to-undo. Teens mis-tap; social deduction rewards reconsideration. Instant commits read as punitive; countdowns read as dramatic.
8. **Named callouts over numeric ratios.** Status copy that drives FOMO names the holdouts ("Waiting on Maya and Lior"), never flatlines to "3 of 7 voted." Numbers are a last-resort fallback when the list outgrows one line.

## Locked Tokens (already shipped — do not redesign without reason)

### Color (`pulse-theme.css`)
- Warm ink ladder: `#15121a` → `#1a1420` → `#1d1826` → `#231c2d` → `#2a2335` (bg → surface tiers). Never pure black.
- Primary accent: **hot pink** `#ff3b6f` (`--pulse-accent`)
- Signal: **gold** `#ffc83d` (`--pulse-gold`) — reserved for silver/premium moments
- Per-cartridge hues (vote=green, game=amber, prompt=blue, dilemma=purple, whisper=violet, nudge=orange) — used sparingly as type-identity, not decoration
- One visible accent at a time. Pink + gold are the 10%.

### Motion (`springs.ts`)
`PULSE_SPRING.bouncy | snappy | gentle | page | pop | exit`. Raw cubic-beziers banned at call sites. Calm by default, event-driven, single ignition beat per moment.

### Icons
Phosphor Icons, **Fill weight**, via `shells/pulse/icons.ts`. Chunky + playful, reads well at small sizes. No emoji.

### Z-index
`PULSE_Z` tiers in `shells/pulse/zIndex.ts`: `base → flow → elevated → popup → reactionBar → drawer → modal → reveal`. Never invent raw numbers.

## Typography (resolved)

Pulse ships on **Clash Display** (display) + **Satoshi** (body), loaded from FontShare.

- **Clash Display** — modular grotesque-display with enough character to read as a reality-TV title card at heading weights. Used for `--po-font-display`: persona names in hero, big reveal labels (Eliminated / Winner), h2 section titles, pill labels.
- **Satoshi** — clean geometric sans with warmth, not IBM-Plex austere. Used for `--po-font-body` and the shell's default `font-family`.

Fallback: `system-ui, sans-serif`. No Outfit in the stack — it was on impeccable's reject list and is now gone.

### Reveal-scale (for climax moments — eliminations, winners, major drama)
- **Hero name**: `clamp(48px, 13vw, 88px)` Clash Display 700 · −0.04em · line-height 0.95. These read "across the room" on a 390px mobile screen.
- **Reveal label** (single dramatic word — OUT / CROWNED / BETRAYED): 20px · 900 · 0.14em tracking. Not 11px tracked-caps; that's whisper-tier, wrong for climax.
- **Mood subtitle** (atmospheric tagline under mechanism name — "Most votes go home", "Fate, not choice"): 14px Clash Display 500 · dim color · not italic.
- **Eyebrow / tracked-caps labels** (for secondary/chrome labels): 11px · 800 · 0.22em. Reserve for labels that shouldn't compete with the moment.

### Accessibility
No formal floor. Keep body-text contrast reasonable (~4.5:1 on warm ink). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for ignition beats. Don't over-constrain decisions for compliance when the product has no compliance target.

## Emergent patterns (from recent cartridge work)

### Bespoke over parameterized for signature moments
When a system has N variants that all need to feel distinct (e.g. the 8 voting mechanisms, the 6 prompt types, the game cartridges), **do not** ship a shared chassis with one branching variable and different tints. That collapses visual identity into an accent-color lottery and reads as generic. Ship distinct geometry per variant — different halo shapes, different entrance choreography, different peak-frame compositions. Shared primitives for chrome (headers, tally grids, avatar pickers) are fine; the *moment itself* wants bespoke.

Concrete reference: `apps/client/src/cartridges/voting/shared/VotingResultHero.tsx` has 7 halo variants (fade / shield / frame / crown / spotlight / burst / rupture), each a different SVG composition with its own entrance. This is the bar.

### Copy voice for dramatic moments
Reality-TV title-card, not enterprise utility. Short. Punchy. Teen-native.
- **Mechanism names** trimmed to one-word where possible: "Majority", "Bubble", "Shield" — not "Majority Vote", "Bubble Vote".
- **Reveal labels** are single dramatic words: Out · Cut · Sunk · Fallen · Betrayed · Crowned. Rendered in uppercase tracked-caps at reveal-scale.
- **Mood subtitles** one short line, scene-setting: "Fate, not choice" · "Saves keep you afloat" · "Trust, or burn it". Every mechanism gets one, system-wide.
- **Rules copy** ("how it works") is always-on, never behind a "?" button. No veterans exist yet; hiding rules costs comprehension.

### Banned visual shortcuts (learned the hard way)
- **Same conic-gradient ring for every "state" variant** — 7+ of the same pattern across components signals a missing design decision. Go bespoke.
- **11px tracked-caps eyebrow labels as the *primary* label for a moment** — whisper-tier typography reading as a caption over a thumbnail, not a title over a hero.
- **Generic `0 6px 24px rgba(0,0,0,0.2)` drop-shadows** — the AI "rounded-rect + drop-shadow generic" tell. Use layered inner-glows tinted to the local accent instead.
- **`box-shadow: inset` side-stripes as a colored accent** — banned outright (see impeccable anti-patterns).
- **Status lines that collapse to "X of N"** — named callouts do the social work (see principle 8).
- **Native `<select>` for persona selection** — personas must be visible; always a custom avatar row/grid.

### Olympic-podium shape
When rendering a top-N row with visual rank (e.g. PodiumRow, leaderboards), **lift #1 up** — don't drop #2/#3 down. Lifting feels triumphal; dropping feels like a list with a gap. Current tokens: `#1: translateY(-10px)` · `#2: -2px` · `#3: 0`.
