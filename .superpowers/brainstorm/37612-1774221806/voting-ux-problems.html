Based on playtest feedback — players confused by mechanics despite explanations

Current Flow

Voting
Bubble Vote

How it works
Each player votes to SAVE one person. The player with the fewest saves is eliminated. You cannot save yourself.
Problem: This disappears after a few seconds. Players forget the rules.

Problems:
1. "Tap a player to vote" — vote to ELIMINATE or SAVE? Bubble = save, but UI says "vote"
2. Tally badge shows "1" — is that saves or eliminate votes?
3. No reminder of rules after splash disappears
4. Can't tell which players are eligible targets vs voters
5. After voting, card vanishes — never see who got eliminated or why

What should change?

Pick the areas that matter most for tomorrow's playtest

1

Persistent Rules Reminder

Collapsible rules section at the top of the voting panel — always accessible, not just in the splash. Shows mechanism name, action verb ("SAVE one player" not just "vote"), and what happens (fewest saves = eliminated). Expanded by default first time, collapsed after.

2

Action-Specific Language

Replace generic "vote" with the actual action: "SAVE a player" (Bubble/Shield), "ELIMINATE a player" (Majority), "ELECT an executioner" (Executioner phase 1), "Pick your target" (Executioner phase 2). Button labels match: "Save Viper" not "Vote for Viper".

3

Full Visual Redesign (frontend-design skill)

Redesign the voting panels from scratch using the frontend-design skill for high design quality. New layout, better visual hierarchy, mechanism-specific theming (Bubble feels different from Executioner), clear status indicators, and polished animations.

4

Rich Result Reveal

When voting ends: dramatic reveal showing full tally (who voted for whom), the eliminated player highlighted, and the result persisting until the next phase. Currently the reveal is a brief flash that vanishes.

5

Live Voter Status

Show who has voted vs who hasn't (persona avatars with checkmark overlays). Players can see participation in real-time. Currently you only see tallies on targets, not whether specific voters have acted.
