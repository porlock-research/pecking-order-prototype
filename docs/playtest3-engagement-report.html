Design Takeaways
💡
What We Learned
Lessons for game balance and scheduling
1. High-stakes mechanics drive engagement

Executioner created a prisoner's dilemma that forced DM scheming. Bubble and Second-to-Last are passive — no drama, no deal-making. Frontload high-engagement voting mechanics early in the game.

2. Losing the "villain" tanks the room

Brenda was 26–44% of all messages. Her elimination removed the main source of conflict. The game needs conflict catalysts — GM provocations or dilemmas when chat goes cold.

3. Ghost players are a design failure

Gary sent 0 messages across Days 3–4 and never voted. Consider AFK detection, silver penalties for inactivity, or auto-elimination after 24 hours of silence.

4. Alternate mechanic intensity

Back-to-back passive mechanics (Bubble then Second-to-Last) compounded the engagement drop. Alternate high-intensity (Executioner, Podium Sacrifice) with passive ones.

5. Silver hot-potato was Day 4's best moment

The only burst of activity came from silver-transfer panic. Financial stakes create interaction. Consider mechanics that force silver decisions more often throughout the day.
