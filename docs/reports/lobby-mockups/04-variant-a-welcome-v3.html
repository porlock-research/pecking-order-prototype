/quieter — stereotype tags

The corner ribbon was the loudest single element on the page. Solid red, white type, drop shadow, top-left over the face. Three problems: covered facial features, drew the eye away from the photo, competed with the headline accent.

Before Red 8px display tracked-caps in corner. SHOWMANCE
After Italic 9px Manrope at 55% white. the showmance. In the name-strip footer, below the name. No bg, no shadow.

Faces stay clean. Stereotype still readable as a quiet caption. The "the [stereotype]" framing borrows from spec/PECKING ORDER.md voice ("The Showmance"), lower-cased here so it reads as quiet attribution, not a header.

/clarify — rhythm verbs

Old verbs named UI mechanisms; new verbs name player intent. Mechanisms ("chat", "play") are how you do things — they should sit in the sub-labels (or just stay implied). The big-type label should be what the player is doing.

Was CHAT — all day
PLAY — a game a day
VOTE — one leaves
Now ALLY — or fake it
EARN — win silver
VOTE — pick who walks

Sub-labels run in italic Manrope to read as captions, not equal-weight info. Three verbs, three captions. Player can scan in two seconds and know what the daily loop demands.

/clarify — temporal framing

You called out the "7 days" copy as inaccurate for DYNAMIC games. Removed. But the slow-async-multi-day signal still has to land — without it, the strip reads like a 5-minute mini-game.

Was "Repeat for 7 days. Last mask standing wins the gold."
Now Eyebrow: "Each day". Footer: "Plays out day after day. Until one mask is left."

Each day + day after day + until one mask is left conveys: it's daily, it repeats, it ends when there's a winner — without committing to a specific length. Works for 5-day, 7-day, or open-ended dynamic games.

What didn't change

Composition skeleton, font choices, masthead, hero copy, form, CTA, verb stack on empty state, footer-line at the bottom of the page — all stable from v2. The two passes only touched what you flagged.

The footer-line was tightened slightly: "Seven days · One winner · Don't get voted out" → "One round a day · One leaves each round · Last mask wins". Same shape, no specific day count.
