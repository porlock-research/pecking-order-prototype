Reality-TV verbs

Old set was clarifying mechanism (Ally / Earn / Vote). New set names the dramatic register of each step.

Was ALLY / EARN / VOTE
Now SCHEME / COMPETE / BETRAY

SCHEME covers alliances + rivalries (the social game). COMPETE is the daily-game step (specifically daily games for silver, since silver is what individuals win even when the group is collaborating for gold). BETRAY is what voting feels like in a social-deduction game — you cut someone you might have schemed with that morning.

Plot dropped because it overlapped scheme. Vote dropped because betray dramatizes it without losing the action.

Catfish, front and center

You called it: catfish is the one word that explains the game on its own. v4 leans into that. The term appears in three places, in red:

1. Dek: "Catfish your friends. Vote them out. Last catfish wins."
2. Rhythm-foot: "Until only one catfish remains."
3. Page footer-line: "Last catfish wins."

Three uses, varied phrasing, same accent color — the eye picks up the through-line without re-reading. Catfish is rendered in the same red as the headline accent and the active rhythm number, so it visually belongs to the same family as the page's most important elements.

Mask, retired

The mask metaphor was a load-bearing concept that didn't have load-bearing follow-through anywhere else in the product. Dropped. Three rewrites:

Was "Pick a mask. Pick a name."
Now No persona-pick framing on /j/ — that's a /join/ activity. Dek leads with the verb (Catfish your friends) instead.
Was "Until one mask is left."
Now "Until only one catfish remains."
Was "Last mask wins"
Now "Last catfish wins"

Real-name copy fixed

Old label said "What should we call you?" with help text "So the group knows you in-game" — both implied the entered name was the in-game identity. It's not. Players see the persona; the real name stays hidden until the reveal.

Was What should we call you?
So the group knows you in-game. Reveal at the end.
Now Your real name
Stays hidden while you play. Other players see your persona. Revealed at the end.

Three things the new help does the old didn't: (1) names what's stored (real name), (2) explains what others see (persona), (3) flags the privacy contract (stays hidden until reveal). The accent on persona hooks back to the persona-pick step on /join/.

Stereotype tags, dropped

Even quietened, the the sweetheart / the bad boy / the showmance lines added another reading layer to a card that's already doing two things (face + name). Per your direction: too noisy. Names alone.

The persona stereotype field is still in the data — it just doesn't surface on the welcome page. The persona pick step (/join/ step 1) keeps the stereotype as the persona's tagline, where it has a job to do (helping the player pick a character that fits). On /j/ the cast is announced; characterization stays for the wizard.

What didn't change

Composition skeleton, fonts, masthead, kicker (Casting Call), headlines (You're invited. / You're in first.), CTA copy (Step in →), verb stack on empty state, cast meta line, palette tokens. The two passes only touched what you flagged.
