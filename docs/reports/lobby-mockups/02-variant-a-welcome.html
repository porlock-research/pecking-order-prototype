Magazine grammar

The page is staged like a tabloid cover: masthead ribbon at the top with issue number + section, photo block as the lede, headline that crops hard, dek beneath. The eye lands top-left, sweeps the photos, drops to the headline.

The ribbon doubles as the invite-code receipt — CODE · ABCD-EFGH rendered in monospace at the right edge so it reads as a tear-off stub. Replaces the redundant "Invite Code: …" line on /join/.

Single accent works hard

Red is the only saturated color, used in three places: the kicker eyebrow, the accent word in the headline, and the primary CTA. Everything else is paper-white or muted gray on ink-black.

Red carries: identity (kicker), emphasis ("in" / "first"), and action (CTA). One color, three roles. No purple-to-pink-to-gold ramp competing for attention.

Cast fan, retuned

Fanned hand-of-cards stays — proven pattern. New: 1.5px white border on each card + small red stereotype tag in the corner (currently rendered as static "S" / etc. in the mockup; would carry the persona's stereotype like Showmance, Quiet One).

Photos get a slight desaturation (filter: contrast(1.1) saturate(0.85)) so faces feel printed, not glossy. The eye reads the cast as a published roster, not a stock-photo grid.

Empty state preserves energy

When you're first in, the cast fan is replaced by the verb stack in alternating white/red — same visual weight as the fan. No boring "no players yet" — the brand mantra IS the empty state.

Plus a "First in · You set the room" tag in red, framing the empty room as privilege, not absence.

Legibility, deterministic

Every text token sits on opaque ground. No photo-bleed-onto-text. Body 14px Manrope at #a8a39c on #0a0a0a — clears WCAG AA easily. Kicker red (#d72638) on ink-black: AAA.

The "First impression. Make it stick." problem on the wizard's step 2 disappears here because the photo never sits behind the copy on /j/. Wizard photo-bg gets its own scrim treatment (covered in the design brief).

Voice preserved

Loud · gossipy · electric stays — just expressed through contrast and type instead of hue:

  • Loud = display-font headlines + tracked-caps eyebrows
  • Gossipy = "Casting Call", magazine ribbon, named cast list
  • Electric = the red accent + shadow-bevel CTA
